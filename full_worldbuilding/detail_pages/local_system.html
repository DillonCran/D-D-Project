Creating your local system!

Star

Stars can come in many different sizes and colours

realistically they can look however you want for your world, but realism is always more believable

In general the bigger the star the shorter lived it is, and the smaller the star the lponger lived. If your world is 10bil years old it would need to be on a smaller star.

Stars can be any colour, but the colour is determined by the temperature of the star. The hotter the star the more blue it is, the cooler the star the more red it is.

Stars can be binary, trinary, or even more. Binary stars are two stars orbiting each other, trinary are three stars orbiting each other, and so on.

However, multiple stars can create whackey orbits. This can create some interesting solar events!

Stars can also be variable, meaning they can change in brightness. This can be due to a number of reasons, but the most common is that the star is a binary star and the other star is blocking the light.

Stars can also be variable due to the star itself. This can be due to the star being young and still forming, or the star is dying and is unstable.

Solar System

Now that you have the central star of your solar system, lets outline the rest of the system

Typical systems have rocky terrestrial planets closer to the star, and large gas giants further away, with smaller exoplanets and belts of comets encircling the outter perimiter

However, your system can be whatever you want it to be. You can have a system with no gas giants, or a system with no terrestrial planets. You can have a system with no exoplanets, or a system with no comets.

Typically, the more planets a system has the more stable it is. This is because the planets can help stabalize each other's orbits. However, this is not always the case. Some systems are very unstable, and some systems are very stable.

Take this oppertunity to outline those stellar objects in rough detail, keeping in mind how they may play into mythology or cosmic events visible from your main world

examples (eclipses, planet alignments, star visual events, moon alignments)

For each stellar object you create, make a rough outline of that they are using stats for size,

size, type(gas,rocky,ice world,etc), orbit speed, orbit position(from the sun), moons, average temp(eath is 14C), planet tilt

General rules of thumb, moon(s) on your main planet should be decently large as large moons stabalize orbits

The tilt of a planet will determine how extreme seasons are, lower tilt means less extreme seasons

There should be 8 - 10 stellar objects in your system

Your Planet

Your main planet, like all the other objects, can be whatever you want regardless of realism

however, if youd like to be more realistic there are some things to consider

the size of your planet will determine the gravity, the larger the planet the more gravity

the tilt of your planet will determine how extreme seasons are, lower tilt means less extreme seasons

The temp of your planet will influince where biomes can be believably placed onto your world.

the orbit of your planet will determine how long a year is, the closer to the sun the shorter the year

the orbit of your planet will also determine how long a day is, the closer to the sun the longer the day

The level of resources is also changeable, does your world have lower oxygen levels? high deposits of whatever magic element you created in the PHYSICS section?
