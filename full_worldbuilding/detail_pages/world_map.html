World Map

Start with a blank map of "all water"

Split the map into continents, drawing 10-30 "plates" connected on the map. 3 plate corners meeting is OK, 4 is not.

Select which plates youve drawn you want to be continental (in the general shap of what you want your continents to look like). The rest will remain oceanic plates.

remember to make sure the left and right sides of the map match up their plates, Its a globe!

The north and south can be covered win polar icecaps for simplicity in map drawing, but its not necissary and you can draw it however you like

Depending on your world temp there may not be any icecaps at all!

Now imagine the continents shifting around along the mantel of your world. Draw arrows for their direction.

Draw the plates as they move, and the land masses as they collide and break apart, creating mountain ranges and others.


How moving continents effects the surface:

3 types of plate coundries:Divergent, Convergent, and Transform

Divergent oceanic plates: Magma rises under the ocean and creates underwater volcanos, maybe volcanic islands

divergent continental plates: Lava rises but will fall below sea level, making inlets/canals between continental plates

Convergent oceanic Plates: One mantel goes under the other, creating ocean trenches and volcanic islands

Convergent continental Plates: Thick continental plates buckle upwards, creating large mountain ranges along the fault

Convergent continental-oceanic plates: Thinner oceanic plate goes under continental plate, creating costal underwater trench with cliff/mountain coastline

Transform: When plates move in parallel directions to another, no real change only earthquakes

The plates will continue to move and change the surface of your world until they reach a balance, and then they will stop moving.

Planet Systems

Once you have the placement of your continents, and finished the movements of the plates to create features

The main systems a planet has are the wind systems and ocean currents.

Wind systems are created by the rotation of the planet, and the ocean currents are created by the wind systems.

If your world rotates in the opposite direction of earth, systems will be flipped

starting from the equator, the water should be flowing right-left. Flowing warm water along any coastline that intersects with its movements (both north and south)

As the current running along the coastline approaches the next set of winds, the water will cool off as it leaves the equator and be pushed by the winds, forming circular patterns of hot water leaving the equator and cool water returning.

Other Features

At this point you can add any other features to your map that you may want. Lakes, rivers, hills, notible metal deposits

Remember that rivers flow from high to low, and will always flow to the ocean. Avoid creating rivrs with large deltas at the mouth

Metal deposits can be located anywhere on the map, but have general outlines for what kinds of metals are found where

Mountains are the best place to find metal deposits, as they are formed by convergent plates

Coal: Deposits are found where low laying swamps USED to contain life, but can be anywhere on a map. Just not inside main mountain chains

Oil & gas: Will naturally seep up into swamps over time, or be trapped underground in rock/salt deposits

Salt: Can be found in salt pans, or places that were once deserts

Gold, Cpper, Tin-Tungston: Will form deposits are typically found along volcanic fault lines with trace amounts of lead-zinc-silver

Iron, Tin, Gold: Found in old mountain chains or swamps, can also rarely have Gold, Cobalt, Magnese, Lead, Silver, Cadmium

Nickle, Platinum, Iriduium: These should be found generally in the older central-basins of larger continents, DIamonds can also be rarely found here

Aluminium & other metals: Put these in your rainforsests and coastal rainy.
