A note on life

Before we lay out the Biomes we should first establish what life looks like on your world

Life is a very broad topic, and we will only be covering the basics here

Life is made up of 3 main kingdoms: Animalia, Plantae, and Fungi

Within the different kingdoms there are varius sub-categories of life

Animalia is broken down into: Mammalia, Reptilia, Amphibia, Insects, and more

Plantae is broken down into: Trees, Shrubs, Grasses, Mosses, Ferns, and more

Fungi is broken down into: Mushrooms, Molds, Yeasts, and more

As we go through the different biomes, please make animals that would be suited to that environment

Biomes

Tropical Rainforests

These are the most biodiverse biomes on the planet. Typically found near the equator and are hot, humid, and recieve lots of rain

For hotter worlds, The tropics move polewards. Colder worlds may not have tropics at all.

Tropics tend to have a wide variety of biodiversity

Almost any kind of animal can be found here, often adapting to climb trees and deal with the heat/humidity

Some animals that live here are: Monkeys, Parrots, Frogs, Snakes, and more

Plants in the tropics often have to comete heavily with eachother, leading to very tall trees with closed canopeys

Often plants will grow on top of other plants in symbiosis, and fungi enjoy the dense forest floor

Think of what kind of fruits/nuts trees may have, or how they have uniquely adapted to the tropics to fill a niche

Some plants that live here are: Trees, Vines, Ferns, and more

Some fungi that live here are: Mushrooms, Molds, and more

Try and make 3x plants, and 3x animals

Savannas

These are the second most biodiverse biomes on the planet. Typically found near the tropics and are hot, dry with little rain.

Typically 1x wet season in the spring, with droughts in the summer/autumn

During droughted summers wildfires can be quite common and often animals migrate between seasons.

Between Savannas and Tropics is a buffer biome called a Monsoon biome that acts as a more humid savanna

Savannas can typically be found north or south of the equator up to the subtropic line

Animals in the Savanna typically either fast, burrowing, or migrate in large herds. Predators are often very fast and have high perception.

Some animals that live here are: Elephants, Giraffes, Lions, Hyenas, and more

Plants in this biome must be adapted for both wildfires and droughts. Trees will not form solid canopeys and are often soletary, with tall grasses and hardy shrubs

Deserts

Deserts can vary widely in appearance, but all have very little percipitation with little vegitation

Between deserts and their neighbouring biomes Steppes provide a rockey buffer biome.

Deserts can be found basically anywhere on the planet, but have a few rules

Rainshadow is the effect when humid air makes land contact where a mountain range is near the coast. The humid air travels up the mountain, losing its humidity beofr falling down the other side of the mountain, creating a rainshadow effect

Deserts can also be found in larger interiors of continents, both hot and cold climates

Colder planets will have more poleward deserts

Water currents also play a roll in desert formation, with desert coastlines being fronted by cold currents.

Plants in the desert will need to adapt to the hot, dry environment.

Some plants that live here are: Cacti, Succulents, Yakka, Tumbleweeds

Animals in the desert will need to adapt to the hot, dry environment as well. Leading to very efficient predators and stealthy/quick prey

Venom is a very common IRL adaptation seen in deserts

sub-tropical

Sub-tropics are found between 30-60 degrees N and S of the equator, following warm water currents

Usually has 1 dry season in summer,spring, or autumn

While it is not as dense of humid as the tropics, many tropical life can live here

Summers are usually very rainy, with 1 season consistant with heavy rain/storms (Tornados, lightning, hurricains)

There will be 4 uniquely distinct seasons

Plants and animals in the sub-tropics will be very similar to the tropics, but will have to deal with the cold winters and sudden storms

Wetlands

Wetlands are found in low-lying areas, often near the coast with sub-tropical biomes

Wetlands are often very humid, with lots of rain and fog. COmbined with flat wide open spaces the environment will get waterlogged

Examples of wetland biomes are Bogs, Marshes, and Peatlands

Wetlands are often very biodiverse, with many animals and plants living in the waterlogged environment

Plants in the wetlands will need to adapt to the waterlogged environment, often with very shallow roots and floating leaves

Some plants that live here are: Lillies, Reeds, Mangroves, rice

Animals in the wetlands will need to adapt to the waterlogged environment as well, often with webbed feet, long beaks, flight, and cold-bloodedness

Some animals that live here are: Ducks, Frogs, Crocodiles, and Lizards

Mediterranian

Mediterranian biomes are found between 30-45 degrees N and S of the equator, following warm water currents

Fair weather, Dry summers, and consistant 4 full seasons

Environment shrub & woodlands, very fertile soil from annual flooding/irrigation

Mediterrainian climates can be hot-warm-cool depending on proximity to poles/warm or cool currents

Plants and animals will be adapted to the fertile soil and seasoned forests

This is the location most likely to create Formable Crops

Some plants that live here are: Olives, Grapes, and Maple

Some animals that live here are: Deer, Rabbits, and Wolves

Oceanic

Oceanic biomes are found 50-75 degrees N and S of the equator

Having a wet, rainy, coastal climate with no dry seasons but warm summers, following warm currents

May also have an abundance of basin-lakes

Environment is often very rocky, with lots of cliffs and mountains

Plants and animals will be adapted to the rocky environment and wet climate

Some plants that live here are: Redwood, rock-pines, Seaweed, and Algae

Some animals that live here are: Seals, Penguins, Squirrels, Raccoons

Highlands

Highland biomes are found in the higher elevations of mountains, in general are rare

Highland biomes are often very cold, with lots of snow and ice, in dry winters and cold summers

Environment is often very rocky, with lots of cliffs and mountains, along plateaus

Animals tend to be scarce in this environment due to the altitude, cold, and low levels of vegitation

Plants in the highlands are often low to the ground, tall seasonal grasses and hardy bush

Continental

Continental biomes are found in the interior of continents with no access to waterways, in general are less common but tend to span large areas

Generally colder/drier, but not much snow or percipitation

typially found along the 60 degree N & S of the equator

Examples of is are Canadian plains, Mongolian steppes

Plant life is often low lying and homogenous, with tall grasses and taigas

Animal life is often very diverse, with large herds of grazing animals and predators

Boreal

Boreal biomes are very similar to Continental biomes, but colder

Hemiboreal is a transisionary biome between boreal and other warmer biomes

Boreal environments tend to be very cold and while not much percipitation snowy

Found in the 60-80 degree N & S of the equator

Examples of this are the Siberian tundra, and the Canadian tundra

Plant life is often very sparse, with low lying grasses and mosses, and taigas

Animal life is often very large to adapt to the cold, with large herds of grazing animals. Typically very furry and scavanging. Hibernation

Arctic

Arctic biomes are found at the N & S poles

Arctic areas are split into two categories, icecap and tundra

Tundra are rocky and cold arctic environments with almost no vegitation. Summer grasses and shrubs. Animals are either predators or small scavangers/semiaquatic

Tundra typically has 1x warm season where snow melts, leaving soggy waterlogged terrain, and rocky

Icecap are frozen wastelands with no vegitation. Animals are either large predators or semiaquatic

Seasons in this biome are varied but will have long dark winters, and permaday

Warmer worlds may not have Icecaps at all, only draw on map if theyre permenant
